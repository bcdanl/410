Course Description

This capstone is an individualized (or small-team) project experience that integrates technical data analytics skills and critical analysis around a topic of interest to the student, potentially connected to a disciplinary area and/or an external organization.

You will work with the capstone instructor to develop an approved proposal. In the proposal, you should demonstrate the ability to carry out data analytics from beginning to end, translate project objectives into a realistic analysis plan, and implement the analysis using tools such as R.

The capstone instructor oversees the data analytics aspects of the project, while a faculty member and/or organization contact may supervise the domain-specific aspects. A written report and an oral presentation are required.

  • Competency Goal 3: Our learners will have effective communications skills.

Integrative and Applied Learning in Data Analytics

This course serves the following Integrative and Applied Learning objectives:

  • Integrate multiple bodies of knowledge with personal experience by asking meaningful questions about real-world problems.
  • Apply skills, theories, and methods gained in academic study, professional experiences, and/or co-curricular experiences to new situations.
  • Reflect upon changes in learning and outlook over time and integrate them into future endeavors.

Course Objectives

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

  • Formulate a focused, meaningful capstone question grounded in a real-world business or socioeconomic context and motivated by stakeholder needs. (CG1, CG4, CG5)
  • Design and execute an end-to-end data analytics project from proposal to final deliverables, including data acquisition, preparation, analysis, validation, and interpretation. (CG1, CG2)
  • Select and apply appropriate quantitative methods (e.g., regression, classification, clustering, hypothesis testing) to answer the project question and evaluate results. (CG1, CG2)
  • Produce publication-quality visualizations and analytical outputs that clearly communicate patterns, uncertainty, and key findings. (CG2, CG3)
  • Demonstrate reproducible and well-documented analytics workflows using modern tools (e.g., R) to ensure transparency and credibility. (CG1, CG2)
  • Integrate relevant domain knowledge (e.g., economics, marketing, finance, accounting, management) to frame the problem, interpret results, and propose actionable recommendations. (CG4)
  • Communicate data-driven insights effectively in written and oral formats for technical and non-technical audiences, including a final report and presentation. (CG3)
  • Identify ethical, social responsibility, and equity considerations related to data, methods, and recommendations (e.g., privacy, bias, fairness, stakeholder impacts). (CG5)

Website

You will build and publish your own course website using Quarto, RStudio, and Git/GitHub. Throughout the semester, you will post reports that include R code, analysis, and visualizations on your Quarto site.

Your website will be hosted on GitHub Pages. We will also cover the basics of Markdown, and introduce essential concepts in HTML and CSS for simple customization.

Capstone Project

Each project team will consist of one or two students. Your team will choose a dataset for the Capstone project, and the dataset must be approved by the instructor before you begin.

The final Capstone write-up must include:

  • Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), including descriptive statistics, data transformation, and multiple visualizations
  • A machine learning analysis such as linear regression, classification, or clustering

All Capstone reports must be published on each team member‚Äôs website. Any change in group membership or project topic must be approved by the instructor.

Attendance & Participation

The knowledge and skills you will gain in this course highly depend on your participation in class learning activities as an in-person class. Because of that, you are expected to attend all class sessions unless you are ill or have a valid reason for missing.

If you are sick or have another valid reason for missing, you must email me before the absence‚Äîany notifications after will be disregarded.

Attendance will be taken during class via a sign-up sheet. You must sign in to be credited for attending class. If you attend class and do not sign in, it will be considered the equivalent of an absence.

You are provided with 5 unexcused absences per semester. Any additional unexcused absences will be subject to reducing Total Percentage Grade by one percentage point for each additional absence (6 total absences = 1% point (90 to 89), 7 total absences = 2% points (90 to 88), etc.).

For extended absences (i.e., more than a couple of days of classes), you should contact the Dean of Students, who can assist with contacting your faculty.

Academic Integrity and Plagiarism

Academic dishonesty includes cheating, knowingly providing false information, plagiarizing, and any other form of academic misrepresentation.

The School of Business regards all acts of cheating and/or plagiarism on tests or any other assignments as unprofessional and unethical behavior that violates College policies as stated in the Student Handbook. Students are expected to be aware of and to obey the College policies concerning academic dishonesty.

Any alleged cheating or plagiarism may be dealt with by the School as a disciplinary problem in accordance with College policies:

https://www.geneseo.edu/handbook/academic-dishonesty-policy

Plagiarism is the representation of someone else‚Äôs words or ideas as one‚Äôs own, or the arrangement of someone else‚Äôs material(s) as one‚Äôs own. In this course, such misrepresentation may be sufficient grounds for a student‚Äôs receiving a grade of E for the paper or presentation involved or may result in an E being assigned as the final grade for the course.

Any one of the following constitutes evidence of plagiarism:

  • Direct quotation without identifying punctuation and citation of source
  • Paraphrase of expression or thought without proper attribution
  • Unacknowledged dependence upon a source in plan, organization, or argument

Use of AI in Coursework

This course encourages you to use Generative AI Tools like ChatGPT or Gemini to support your work. To maintain academic integrity, you must disclose and properly attribute any AI-generated material you use, including in-text citations, quotations, and references.

Guidance for citing AI-generated content is available at:

https://apastyle.apa.org/blog/how-to-cite-chatgpt

In cases where I discover the use of generative AI without proper citation, these cases will be treated as instances of academic dishonesty and will be subject to the processes outlined in the SUNY Geneseo Academic Dishonesty Policy.

Generative AI Statement: Use of generative AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini) must be disclosed and cited. Unauthorized use may violate academic integrity.
